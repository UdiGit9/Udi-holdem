How this trainer works

Practice vs. Test

Practice lets you pick one of the six scenarios below and drill it with unlimited hands — no timer, no test counter, just running accuracy.

Test gives you 20 mixed hands (randomly drawn across all six scenarios). When you finish, you can add your score to the weekly leaderboard — it shows the top 10 scores from the past 7 days. Names you've used before will appear as suggestions when you save your next score.

Preflop — No action before you (RFI)

Action folds to you. The decision is open-raise or fold. Calling (limping) into an unopened pot isn't a GTO action.

Preflop — Facing an open-raise

A player has opened to 2.5bb. 3-bet with strongest + bluff blockers, call with middling-to-strong hands that flop well, or fold.

Flop — You opened, BB called

Decision: c-bet or check. Range-bet small on high-card dry boards; bet value + strong draws on wet textures; check marginal hands on connected boards.

Flop — Facing a c-bet (you called in BB)

Continue with pairs+, flush draws, open-enders, and strong gutshots; raise with sets/two-pair on wet boards; fold unpaired air.

Turn — You opened, BB called flop

Barrel with top-pair+, overpairs, sets, strong draws. Check-back marginal hands for pot control and range protection.

River — You opened, BB called flop + turn

Value-bet top-pair-good-kicker+; check marginal / missed draws for showdown value.

About the "correct" answers

Preflop ranges approximate published solver-derived GTO charts at 100bb. Postflop decisions use hand-written heuristics that mimic the broad conclusions solvers reach (range advantage, texture-based sizing, polarization). This is a pattern-recognition tool, not an exact solver — some marginal spots where a real solver would mix will be marked "wrong" here.
